Why Knowing Your Numbers Matters

According to the CDC and American Heart Association: Over 120 million American adults have high blood pressure, but only 1 in 4 have it under control. Nearly 94 million adults over age 20 have high cholesterol. These "silent killers" often have no symptoms but dramatically increase your risk of heart disease, stroke, and premature death. Student-athletes face unique challenges with athletic heart syndrome, dehydration, and performance optimization requiring specialized monitoring.

National Health Statistics & Trends

Cardiovascular Disease Burden (CDC 2024)

  • 697,000 deaths from heart disease annually (1 in 5 deaths)
  • $239 billion annual cost of cardiovascular disease
  • 122.4 million adults with hypertension
  • 6.9 million adults with coronary heart disease
  • 795,000 strokes occur each year

Cholesterol Statistics (American Heart Association 2024)

  • 94 million US adults age 20+ have high cholesterol (‚â•200 mg/dL)
  • 28 million adults could benefit from cholesterol medication
  • 38% of adults have high cholesterol
  • Only 55% of eligible adults receive treatment

Obesity Epidemic (NIH & CDC Data)

  • 42.4% of US adults are obese (BMI ‚â• 30)
  • 73.6% of adults are overweight or obese (BMI ‚â• 25)
  • 19.7% of children and adolescents (ages 2-19) are obese
  • $173 billion annual medical costs related to obesity

Student-Athlete Specific Data

  • 1 in 50,000 to 1 in 80,000 young athletes experience sudden cardiac death annually
  • Athletic Heart Syndrome: 40-80% of endurance athletes show cardiac adaptations
  • Female Athlete Triad: Affects up to 60% of female athletes (energy deficiency, menstrual dysfunction, bone loss)
  • Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy: Leading cause of sudden cardiac death in athletes
  • Mental Health: 30% of student-athletes report anxiety, 25% depression
